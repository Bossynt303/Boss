Equation Inequality

An equation is a mathematical statement that asserts the equality of two expressions, which are connected by an equal sign (=). For example,x+3=7x+3=7 is an equation, where x is a variable. 
An inequality, on the other hand, is a mathematical statement that compares two expressions and indicates their relationship using inequality symbols such as<< (less than),>> (greater than),≤≤ (less than or equal to), or≥≥ (greater than or equal to). For example,x+3<7x+3<7 is an inequality.

Types of Inequalities:

Linear Inequalities: These are inequalities where each term is either a constant or the product of a constant and a single variable raised to the first power. Examples include:
2x + 3 < 7
4x −5 ≥ 3x + 2
Quadratic Inequalities: These are inequalities involving quadratic expressions. For example:
x^2 − 4x +4 >0
2x^2 +3x −5 ≤0
Systems of Inequalities: These are a set of inequalities that are solved simultaneously. For example:
2x + x ≤ 8
x − y >2

Solving Equations and Inequalities

Equations: To solve an equation, you typically use algebraic methods to isolate the variable on one side of the equation. The goal is to find the value(s) of the variable that make the equation true.
Inequalities: Solving inequalities involves finding the range of values for the variable that satisfy the inequality. Depending on the type of inequality, you might use algebraic methods similar to those used for equations, or you might use graphical methods to represent the solution set on a number line or coordinate plane.
