Geometry

Geometry is a branch of mathematics that studies the sizes, shapes, positions, angles, and dimensions of things.

Types of Geometry

Geometry consisted of many types. For example : Planar Surface, Spherical Surface, Hyperbolic Surface. 

Shapes

Polygons:
Classifying polygons (triangles, quadrilaterals, etc.) based on their number of sides and properties like sum of interior and exterior angles.
Triangles:
Understanding different triangle types (scalene, isosceles, equilateral) based on their side and angle measures. Applying the Pythagorean Theorem to solve problems involving right triangles.
Quadrilaterals:
Properties of special quadrilaterals like squares, rectangles, parallelograms, and trapezoids. 

Transformations and Measurement

Similarity and Congruence: Properties of similar shapes (proportional sides, corresponding angles) and criteria for proving triangles congruent (SSS, SAS, AAS).
Coordinate Geometry: Introduction to the coordinate plane, plotting points using coordinates, and using the distance formula and midpoint formula.
Area and Volume: Formulas for calculating areas of common 2D shapes and volumes of basic 3D solids (prisms, pyramids, cones, cylinders, spheres). You might also learn about surface area of 3D shapes.

Formulars

Distance and Midpoint
Distance Formula: This formula calculates the distance between two points with coordinates (x1, y1) and (x2, y2) on a coordinate plane. Distance = √[(x2 - x1)² + (y2 - y1)²]
Midpoint Formula: This formula finds the midpoint coordinates of a line segment given the coordinates of its endpoints (x1, y1) and (x2, y2). Midpoint coordinates = ((x1 + x2) / 2, (y1 + y2) / 2)
Lines and Angles
Linear Equation of a Line: This equation expresses the relationship between the x and y coordinates of points on a straight line. It can be in slope-intercept form (y = mx + b) or point-slope form (y - y1 = m(x - x1)) where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept.
Triangles
Pythagorean Theorem: This theorem relates the sides of a right triangle. It states that a² + b² = c², where a and b are the lengths of the legs (sides opposite and adjacent to the right angle), and c is the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle). Area of a Triangle: This formula calculates the area of a triangle. Area = ½ * base * height, where base is the length of any side of the triangle and height is the perpendicular distance from that base to the opposite vertex.
Quadrilaterals
Area of a Rectangle: This formula finds the area of a rectangle. Area = length * width, where length and width are the dimensions of the rectangle.
Area of a Parallelogram: Similar to a rectangle, the area of a parallelogram is calculated by multiplying its base by the height. Area = base * height
Circles
Circumference of a Circle: This formula calculates the distance around the circle. Circumference = 2πr, where r is the circle's radius and π (pi) is a mathematical constant approximately equal to 3.14.
Area of a Circle: This formula finds the area enclosed by the circle. Area = πr², where r is the circle's radius and π (pi) is a mathematical constant.
Volume and Surface Area
Volume of a Prism: This formula calculates the volume of a prism (rectangular or triangular). Volume = base area * depth, where base area is the area of the prism's base and depth is the distance between the parallel faces.
Volume of a Cylinder: This formula finds the volume of a cylinder. Volume = πr²h, where r is the cylinder's radius, h is its height, and π (pi) is a mathematical constant.
Volume of a Cone: This formula calculates the volume of a cone. Volume = (1/3)πr²h, where r is the cone's radius, h is its height, and π (pi) is a mathematical constant.
Volume of a Sphere: This formula finds the volume of a sphere. Volume = (4/3)πr³, where r is the sphere's radius and π (pi) is a mathematical constant.
